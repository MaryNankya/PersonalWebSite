Robin Chataut is an Assistant Professor of Computer Science at Fitchburg State University. He obtained his Ph.D. in Computer Science and Engineering from the University of North Texas, Texas, USA, in 2020 and his undergraduate degree in Electronics and Communication Engineering from Pulchowk Campus, Tribhuvan University, Nepal in 2014. Prior to completing his Ph.D., he was a senior software developer, designing android apps. His research interests are in the areas of wireless communication and networks, vehicular communication, smart cities, Internet of Things, wireless sensor networks, and network security. He has designed, implemented, and optimized several algorithms and hardware architectures for precoding, detection, user scheduling, channel estimation, and pilot contamination mitigation for massive MIMO systems. He has authored and co-authored several research articles. He has taught various courses in Computer Science and Engineering and mentored undergraduate/graduate students and at UNT and FSU. He is an active reviewer in several international scientific journals and conferences. He is also member of IEEE, IEEE future Networks and future networks committee.
